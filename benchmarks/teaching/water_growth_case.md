# Benchmark Case — Water Growth Teaching Slide

## Scenario

Undergraduate teaching presentation.

Topic:
How water affects crop growth.

Audience:
undergraduate students with limited agricultural background.

Presentation goal:
help students intuitively understand
the causal relationship between water and crop growth.

---

# Skill Routing

Primary skill:
- teaching_skill

Secondary skill:
- academic_skill

Reason:
The slide requires:
- teaching clarity
- simplified scientific mechanism explanation

---

# Cognitive Objective

Students should understand:

water does not only "supply water".

Water influences:
- root absorption
- stomatal opening
- photosynthesis
- dry matter accumulation
- final yield formation

The audience should understand
the mechanism chain clearly within several seconds.

---

# Narrative Structure

Preferred logic:

Soil Water
→ Root Absorption
→ Stomatal Opening
→ Photosynthesis
→ Dry Matter Accumulation
→ Yield Formation

Additional comparison:

Water Deficiency
→ Stomatal Closure
→ Reduced Photosynthesis
→ Growth Limitation

---

# Slide Type

Mechanism explanation slide.

Layout type:
horizontal progressive mechanism flow.

---

# Visual Semantic Strategy

Primary colors:
- green
- blue

Reason:
green:
- agriculture
- crop growth

blue:
- water
- biological process

Orange:
used ONLY for stress pathway emphasis.

---

# Cognitive Load Rules

The slide should:
- minimize text density
- maximize mechanism readability
- emphasize directional flow
- use clear arrows
- reduce decorative elements

Audience should understand:
within 3 seconds.

---

# Expected Visual Structure

Top:
slide title + concise mechanism subtitle

Center:
large horizontal mechanism chain

Bottom:
stress comparison pathway

Final takeaway:
water influences crop growth
through physiological mechanisms,
not simple irrigation alone.

---

# Expected Output Characteristics

The generated slide should:
- feel educational
- feel intuitive
- feel mechanism-centered
- avoid academic overload
- avoid decorative infographic style

The slide should support:
- classroom explanation
- verbal teaching
- rapid audience understanding
