# Benchmark Case — Faculty Interview Presentation

## Scenario

Faculty recruitment presentation.

Candidate background:
Agricultural Water and Soil Engineering
+ Digital Agriculture
+ Spatial Information Technology

Audience:
- faculty recruitment committee
- professors
- academic reviewers

Presentation goal:
establish professional credibility,
demonstrate interdisciplinary positioning,
and communicate future academic potential.

---

# Skill Routing

Primary skill:
- interview_skill

Secondary skill:
- academic_skill

Reason:
The presentation requires:
- strategic communication
- scientific credibility
- professional positioning

---

# Cognitive Objective

Audience should quickly understand:

the candidate is:
- interdisciplinary
- application-oriented
- scientifically credible
- suitable for modern agricultural engineering development

The presentation should amplify:
- digital agriculture integration
- mechanism-to-application capability
- engineering practicality

Avoid exposing:
- excessive theoretical confrontation
- unsupported deep theory claims

---

# Narrative Strategy

Preferred structure:

Background
→ Research Direction
→ Representative Research
→ Scientific Logic
→ Teaching Philosophy
→ Future Research Plan
→ Institutional Value

Narrative should feel:
- coherent
- progressive
- professionally controlled

---

# Strategic Communication Rules

Prioritize:
- credibility
- clarity
- professional restraint
- structured communication

Avoid:
- excessive self-promotion
- visually emotional storytelling
- startup pitch aesthetics
- overloaded scientific density

---

# Visual Semantic Strategy

Primary colors:
- dark blue
- green
- white

Blue:
- professionalism
- scientific systems
- digital technology

Green:
- agriculture
- ecology
- sustainability

White/gray:
- academic restraint
- credibility
- cognitive comfort

---

# Cognitive Control Rules

Each slide should:
- communicate one strategic message
- reinforce professional identity
- reduce cognitive ambiguity

Audience should:
understand the candidate's positioning
within several seconds.

---

# Expected Visual Structure

Top:
clear academic title

Center:
structured scientific narrative
with concise mechanism support

Bottom:
core takeaway
or strategic conclusion

Visual hierarchy should feel:
- clean
- restrained
- professional
- academically trustworthy

---

# Expected Output Characteristics

The generated presentation should:
- feel academically mature
- feel strategically controlled
- feel cognitively coherent
- feel institutionally appropriate

Avoid:
- excessive decoration
- aggressive layouts
- presentation-template aesthetics

The presentation should support:
- faculty interviews
- academic job talks
- university recruitment presentations
