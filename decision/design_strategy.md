# Design Strategy Decision Engine

This engine determines high-level presentation strategy based on:

- scenario
- audience
- presentation objective
- cognitive constraints
- risk factors

The goal is to convert presentation conditions into presentation strategy.

---

# Core Principle

Different presentation contexts require different presentation intelligence strategies.

There is NO universal slide style.

---

# Strategy Dimensions

The engine should determine:

- professionalism level
- visual density
- explanation depth
- narrative pacing
- visual complexity
- diagram frequency
- text compression level
- animation necessity
- emotional tone

---

# Teaching-oriented Strategy

If the scenario is:

- undergraduate teaching
- classroom presentation
- teaching interview

Then prioritize:

- simplified explanation
- progressive cognition
- diagram-driven slides
- low cognitive load
- visual guidance
- intuitive storytelling

Reduce:

- formula density
- academic abstraction
- excessive terminology

---

# Academic-defense Strategy

If the scenario is:

- master defense
- PhD defense
- scientific conference

Then prioritize:

- logical rigor
- methodology clarity
- figure-based explanation
- professional hierarchy
- evidence structure

Reduce:

- decorative design
- excessive animation
- storytelling exaggeration

---

# Business-presentation Strategy

If the scenario is:

- business pitch
- product presentation
- commercial storytelling

Then prioritize:

- emotional rhythm
- visual impact
- persuasion structure
- audience engagement
- concise messaging

Reduce:

- excessive technical depth
- dense explanations

---

# High-risk Presentation Strategy

If the system detects:

- strong questioning risk
- expert audience
- highly technical content

Then:

- reduce decorative elements
- strengthen logical hierarchy
- increase visual clarity
- simplify explanations
- improve evidence visibility

---

# Cognitive-density Strategy

If audience expertise is LOW:

- reduce information density
- increase whitespace
- increase diagrams
- reduce terminology
- slow narrative pacing

If audience expertise is HIGH:

- allow higher density
- increase technical precision
- allow complex figures
- reduce explanatory redundancy

---

# Visual Strategy Rules

High-density slides require:

- stronger hierarchy
- more whitespace
- controlled color usage
- stronger alignment

Low-density slides require:

- visual rhythm
- semantic imagery
- emotional pacing

---

# Final Principle

Presentation strategy should adapt to:

audience cognition

rather than

presenter preference.
