# Presentation Revision Workflow

## Runtime Identity

This workflow is NOT:

- a slide editing pipeline
- a redesign engine
- a visual correction workflow
- a template modification system
- an infographic optimization pipeline

This workflow IS:

a cognition refinement runtime
for orchestrating
iterative communication stabilization.

The objective is NOT:

continuous redesign.

The objective IS:

localized cognition correction
with hierarchy preservation,
semantic continuity,
workflow stability,
and runtime self-correction.

Presentation revision should improve:

- hierarchy clarity
- mechanism interpretability
- teaching accessibility
- professional stability
- cognitive readability
- communication effectiveness

while preserving:

- presentation identity
- narrative continuity
- visual atmosphere
- audience adaptation
- strategic intent

Revision should feel:

intentional,
minimal,
and cognitively grounded.

---

# Core Workflow Philosophy

Presentation revision is NOT:

visual modification.

It IS:

cognitive communication correction.

The workflow must determine:

- what communication failed
- why the failure occurred
- what hierarchy collapsed
- what audience friction appeared
- what refinement strategy is required

Then:

perform the smallest correction
with the largest communication improvement.

Avoid:

- unnecessary regeneration
- unrelated redesign
- visual personality drift
- narrative destabilization

Revision should preserve:

presentation coherence.

# Stage 7 — Revision Strategy Selection

The workflow must determine:

what refinement strategy
produces the highest communication gain.

---

## Density Reduction Strategy

Use when:

- slides feel crowded
- mechanisms become unreadable
- attention becomes unstable

Preferred actions:

- reduce secondary information
- enlarge dominant visuals
- simplify annotations
- increase whitespace

Avoid:

adding more structure
to overloaded layouts.

---

## Scientific Enhancement Strategy

Use when:

- slides feel visually weak
- scientific credibility decreases
- mechanisms lose interpretability

Preferred actions:

- strengthen mechanism hierarchy
- improve evidence linkage
- stabilize editorial structure
- reinforce scientific calmness

Avoid:

decorative scientific redesign.

---

## Teaching Accessibility Strategy

Use when:

- learners struggle
- terminology becomes excessive
- abstraction overwhelms cognition

Preferred actions:

- enlarge diagrams
- simplify wording
- strengthen visual guidance
- reduce simultaneous concepts

Avoid:

oversimplification
that removes scientific meaning.

---

## Professional Stabilization Strategy

Use when:

- presentations feel visually aggressive
- professionalism weakens
- academic calmness disappears

Preferred actions:

- stabilize typography
- reduce decorative styling
- simplify hierarchy
- enlarge whitespace rhythm

Avoid:

startup-style redesign behavior.
