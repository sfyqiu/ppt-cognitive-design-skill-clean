# Audience Persona System

This system defines how presentation intelligence should adapt to different audience personas.

Presentation quality depends heavily on:

audience adaptation.

Different audiences require different:

- cognitive density
- narrative pacing
- terminology complexity
- visual hierarchy
- explanation strategy

There is NO universal presentation style.

---

# Core Principle

Presentation design should optimize for:

audience cognition

NOT:

presenter preference.

---

# 1. Undergraduate Student Persona

## Characteristics

- limited technical depth
- lower abstraction tolerance
- short attention span
- strong visual dependency
- requires progressive explanation

## Recommended Strategy

- intuitive storytelling
- diagram-driven explanation
- low terminology density
- strong visual hierarchy
- progressive cognition

## Avoid

- formula overload
- dense academic language
- abstract-first explanation

---

# 2. Graduate Student Persona

## Characteristics

- moderate technical expertise
- moderate abstraction tolerance
- interested in methodology
- stronger logical expectations

## Recommended Strategy

- mechanism explanation
- moderate technical depth
- balanced visuals and logic
- methodological clarity

## Avoid

- excessive simplification
- weak scientific rigor

---

# 3. Expert Academic Persona

## Characteristics

- high technical expertise
- high questioning intensity
- low tolerance for weak logic
- focuses on contribution and rigor

## Recommended Strategy

- strong hierarchy
- evidence-driven explanation
- concise scientific communication
- restrained visual style
- methodology transparency

## Avoid

- decorative storytelling
- exaggerated simplification
- unsupported claims

---

# 4. Interdisciplinary Academic Persona

## Characteristics

- mixed disciplinary backgrounds
- uneven technical familiarity
- requires conceptual accessibility
- values broader significance

## Recommended Strategy

- interdisciplinary storytelling
- conceptual explanation
- semantic diagrams
- simplified abstraction
- mechanism visualization

## Avoid

- discipline-specific jargon overload
- isolated technical detail

---

# 5. Business Executive Persona

## Characteristics

- decision-oriented
- limited attention span
- low tolerance for complexity
- high focus on value and outcomes

## Recommended Strategy

- concise messaging
- persuasive hierarchy
- rapid value communication
- strong emotional rhythm
- executive readability

## Avoid

- excessive methodology explanation
- dense analytical slides

---

# 6. Teaching Interview Committee Persona

## Characteristics

- evaluates professionalism
- evaluates teaching ability
- evaluates communication clarity
- sensitive to presentation risk

## Recommended Strategy

- balanced professionalism
- strong teaching clarity
- controlled complexity
- stable visual hierarchy
- interdisciplinary positioning

## Avoid

- excessive technical claims
- over-complicated mechanisms
- unstable narrative pacing

---

# 7. Public / Non-specialist Persona

## Characteristics

- minimal technical background
- highly visual cognition
- low abstraction tolerance
- emotionally driven understanding

## Recommended Strategy

- storytelling-first explanation
- visual simplification
- intuitive examples
- strong semantic visuals
- emotional accessibility

## Avoid

- scientific terminology overload
- dense logic chains
- technical abstraction

---

# Persona Adaptation Rules

The system should dynamically adjust:

- terminology complexity
- explanation depth
- visual density
- narrative pacing
- diagram frequency
- cognitive compression

based on audience persona.

---

# Final Principle

A presentation succeeds when:

the audience feels the presentation was designed specifically for them.
