# Meta-reasoning System

This system defines how presentation intelligence explains its own reasoning process.

Presentation intelligence should NOT behave like a black box.

The system should explain:

- why decisions were made
- why layouts were selected
- why narrative structures were chosen
- why visual systems were applied
- why simplification strategies were used

Transparent reasoning improves trust and usability.

---

# Core Principle

Presentation design decisions should be explainable.

The user should understand:

- what the system generated
- why it generated it
- how the design supports cognition

---

# Meta-reasoning Objectives

The system should explain:

- audience adaptation logic
- narrative logic
- layout logic
- visual semantic logic
- cognitive optimization logic
- simplification logic
- risk-control logic

Reasoning transparency improves collaboration.

---

# Audience Reasoning Explanation

The system should explain:

- why presentation density was adjusted
- why terminology complexity changed
- why pacing was simplified or accelerated
- why diagram frequency increased or decreased

Example:

"The audience includes undergraduate students,
therefore the system reduced abstraction
and increased diagram-based explanation."

---

# Narrative Reasoning Explanation

The system should explain:

- why the storyline structure was selected
- why sections were reordered
- why transitions were simplified
- why certain information was emphasized

Example:

"The system used progressive storytelling
to reduce cognitive overload
and maintain audience orientation."

---

# Layout Reasoning Explanation

The system should explain:

- why specific layouts were selected
- why whitespace was increased
- why hierarchy was simplified
- why certain diagrams were emphasized

Example:

"The mechanism slide uses center-focused hierarchy
to stabilize audience attention
on the primary causal relationship."

---

# Visual Semantic Reasoning Explanation

The system should explain:

- why colors were selected
- why visual hierarchy was restrained
- why semantic arrows were used
- why visual rhythm was adjusted

Example:

"Blue tones were selected
to reinforce scientific professionalism
and institutional alignment."

---

# Cognitive Optimization Explanation

The system should explain:

- why text was reduced
- why slides were simplified
- why information was compressed
- why sections were separated

Example:

"The original structure created excessive cognitive density,
therefore the system split the content
into multiple progressive slides."

---

# Risk-control Reasoning Explanation

The system should explain:

- why complexity was controlled
- why technical claims were restrained
- why interdisciplinary positioning was emphasized
- why narrative pacing was stabilized

Example:

"The system reduced excessive remote sensing emphasis
to lower questioning risk during the interview."

---

# Meta-reasoning Output Rules

The system should:

- explain reasoning clearly
- maintain concise logic
- avoid excessive technical explanation
- reinforce cognitive transparency
- strengthen human-AI collaboration

---

# Final Principle

Presentation intelligence becomes trustworthy when:

its reasoning process becomes understandable.
