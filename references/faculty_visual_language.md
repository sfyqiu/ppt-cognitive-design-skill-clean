# Faculty Visual Language System

## Purpose

This document defines the faculty interview
visual language system
for AI-native presentation generation.

The objective is NOT:
visually impressive academic slides.

The objective IS:
strategic academic communication.

Faculty interview visuals should help audiences:

- trust the presenter
- understand academic positioning
- perceive future potential
- follow research logic calmly
- evaluate professionalism efficiently

Strong faculty visuals prioritize:

clarity,
professional calmness,
and
strategic maturity.

---

# Core Philosophy

Faculty interview presentations are NOT:
publication collections.

They are:

academic positioning systems.

Faculty visuals should communicate:

- research direction
- interdisciplinary value
- teaching capability
- scientific maturity
- institutional compatibility

Strong faculty presentations feel:

calm,
credible,
and professionally intentional.

---

# Global Faculty Principle

Faculty slides should establish:

Academic positioning
↓
Research framework
↓
Supporting evidence
↓
Future direction

The audience should rapidly identify:

- who the candidate is
- what research direction matters
- what future trajectory exists
- why this candidate fits the institution

Avoid:

- publication dumping
- achievement stacking
- dense scientific overload
- self-promotional slide behavior

---

# Strategic Communication Principle

Faculty visuals should support:

institutional trust.

Preferred communication feeling:

- mature
- restrained
- stable
- intellectually confident

Avoid:

- aggressive consulting aesthetics
- startup pitch behavior
- corporate dashboard systems
- visually intimidating scientific density

Strong faculty presentations feel:

approachable
and
professionally composed.

---

# Editorial Academic Principle

Faculty slides should resemble:

editorial academic communication.

Preferred characteristics:

- stable hierarchy
- calm typography
- restrained spacing
- moderate density
- intentional pacing

Avoid:

- decorative infographic systems
- overloaded visual effects
- excessive icon dependency
- flashy transitions

Academic maturity should emerge from:

clarity,
not decoration.

---

Faculty slides should prioritize:

academic messages.

Preferred hierarchy:

Core message
↓
Supporting framework
↓

↓
Interpretation

- evidence-first slides

- publication screenshot collections

the research vision,
not the slide density.

# Final Validation

Before finalizing:

Check:

- Does the presentation feel professionally calm?
- Is academic positioning obvious?
- Is research direction interpretable?
- Is hierarchy stable?
- Is whitespace sufficient?
- Does the presentation feel institutionally mature?
- Does the visual language avoid AI presentation clichés?

If NO:

simplify,
rebalance,
and reduce aggression.
