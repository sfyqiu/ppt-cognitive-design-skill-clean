# Teaching Presentation Case

## Scenario

Teaching demonstration presentation.

Topic:
How salinity affects crop growth.

Audience:
First-year undergraduate students.

Presentation Goal:
Help students intuitively understand
the relationship between
soil salinity and crop stress.

---

# Routing Result

Primary skill:
teaching_skill

Secondary skill:
interview_skill

Primary style:
teaching_clean

Cognitive density:
low

Visual strategy:
diagram-centered explanation

---

# Narrative Structure

1. What is salinity?
2. Why does salinity matter?
3. How salinity affects roots
4. How crops respond to stress
5. Typical field symptoms
6. Basic mitigation methods
7. Summary

Narrative should feel:

- progressive
- visual
- easy to follow
- teaching-oriented

---

# Slide Planning Example

## Slide Type

Principle slide

---

## Slide Goal

Help students understand
how salinity reduces water uptake.

---

## Key Message

High salt concentration
makes water absorption difficult.

---

## Visual Focus

Root-water relationship diagram.

---

## Layout Strategy

Left:
simplified explanation

Right:
annotated mechanism figure

---

## Cognitive Strategy

Use:
- arrows
- color contrast
- simplified labels
- minimal terminology

Avoid:
- equations
- dense text
- scientific jargon

---

# Revision Example

User feedback:

"The slide feels too academic.
Students may not understand."

---

# Revision Interpretation

Possible problems:

- excessive terminology
- weak visualization
- high cognitive density

---

# Revision Actions

AI should:

- simplify wording
- enlarge diagrams
- reduce text blocks
- strengthen visual explanation
- add progressive annotations

Avoid:

- adding more scientific details
- increasing layout complexity

---

# Expected Result

The revised slide should feel:

- clearer
- more intuitive
- visually guided
- cognitively lighter

Students should understand
the core mechanism quickly.

---

# Case Philosophy

A strong teaching presentation
does not maximize information.

It maximizes understanding.
