# Interview Presentation Case

## Scenario

Faculty interview presentation.

Topic:
Integration of digital agriculture
and agricultural water-soil engineering.

Audience:
Faculty interview committee.

Presentation Goal:
Demonstrate research potential,
teaching ability,
and interdisciplinary positioning.

---

# Routing Result

Primary skill:
interview_skill

Secondary skill:
academic_skill

Primary style:
faculty_interview

Cognitive density:
medium-low

Visual strategy:
professional academic communication

---

# Narrative Structure

1. Personal introduction
2. Research direction
3. Representative mechanism studies
4. Teaching philosophy
5. Future research plan
6. Institutional fit
7. Closing vision

Narrative should feel:

- stable
- professional
- balanced
- approachable
- growth-oriented

---

Research contribution slide

The candidate combines:

research themes and relationships

## Cognitive Strategy

Use:
- concise scientific language
- stable hierarchy
- restrained colors
- clear structure

Avoid:
- exaggerated claims
- excessive text
- crowded layouts
- overly decorative visuals

---

# Revision Example

User feedback:

"The presentation feels too academic.
Teaching ability is not obvious enough."

---

# Revision Interpretation

Possible problems:

- research density too high
- insufficient teaching communication
- weak audience accessibility

---

# Revision Actions

AI should:

- simplify scientific explanation
- strengthen teaching-oriented visuals
- increase intuitive diagrams
- reduce terminology density
- improve communication friendliness

Avoid:

- removing scientific credibility
- redesigning the whole presentation
- excessive simplification

---

# Expected Result

The revised presentation should feel:

- more approachable
- professionally balanced
- teaching-capable
- strategically credible

The interview committee should feel:

- this candidate can communicate
- this candidate can teach
- this candidate has growth potential

---

# Case Philosophy

A strong interview presentation
does not attempt to impress
through complexity.

It creates:

trust,
clarity,
and future potential.
