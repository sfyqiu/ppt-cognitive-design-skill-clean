# Teaching Interview Presentation Case

## Original User Requirement

The user needed to prepare a teaching interview presentation for a university faculty position.

Presentation requirements included:

- self-introduction
- research background
- scientific achievements
- teaching demonstration
- limited presentation duration
- interview questioning session

The target institution included majors related to:

- GIS
- remote sensing
- land resource management
- surveying and mapping

The user's actual expertise was:

- agricultural engineering
- agricultural water-soil engineering
- digital agriculture

The user had limited confidence in pure remote sensing expertise.

---

# Initial System Reasoning

## Scenario Identification

The system identified the scenario as:

# teaching interview presentation

This implied:

- high professionalism requirement
- moderate cognitive density
- strong teaching emphasis
- low tolerance for confusion
- interview-risk sensitivity

---

## Audience Reasoning

Target audience included:

- interview committee
- professors
- interdisciplinary faculty
- potential teaching evaluators

The audience was expected to:

- rapidly judge professionalism
- evaluate teaching clarity
- evaluate discipline compatibility

---

## Risk Identification

Potential risks included:

- excessive remote sensing depth
- over-claiming technical expertise
- high questioning risk
- excessive academic complexity
- weak teaching orientation

  agricultural engineering + digital agriculture + spatiotemporal information
