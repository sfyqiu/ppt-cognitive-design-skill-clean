# Presentation Revision Case

## Scenario

A scientific presentation draft
has already been generated.

The user reviews slides one by one
and provides iterative feedback.

The system should support:

- local refinement
- cognitive alignment
- presentation stabilization
- iterative collaboration

NOT unnecessary regeneration.

---

# Initial Presentation Context

Presentation type:
Scientific conference presentation.

Topic:
Rhizosphere microenvironment regulation
under membrane-controlled irrigation.

Audience:
Researchers and graduate students.

Primary skill:
academic_skill

Secondary skill:
teaching_skill

Primary style:
sci_top_journal

---

# Revision Example 1

## User Feedback

"Slide 4 feels too dense.
The mechanism is difficult to follow."

---

## Revision Interpretation

Possible problems:

- excessive information density
- weak mechanism hierarchy
- competing focal points
- insufficient reading guidance

---

## Revision Actions

AI should:

- simplify mechanism chain
- reduce secondary annotations
- increase whitespace
- strengthen directional flow
- emphasize one core mechanism

Avoid:

- redesigning unrelated slides
- adding more scientific terminology
- increasing visual complexity

---

## Expected Result

The revised slide should feel:

- cleaner
- easier to interpret
- more mechanism-oriented
- visually guided

Audience should quickly identify:

- the mechanism
- the causal relationship
- the scientific takeaway

# Revision Example 2

## User Feedback

"The slide does not feel academic enough."

---

## Revision Interpretation

Possible problems:

- weak scientific hierarchy
- excessive decorative feeling
- insufficient evidence linkage
- unstable typography

---

## Revision Actions

AI should:

- strengthen scientific structure
- improve evidence hierarchy
- reduce decorative elements
- stabilize typography
- increase mechanism interpretation

Avoid:

- excessive redesign
- overcrowding the slide
- exaggerated scientific styling

---

## Expected Result

The revised slide should feel:

- more professional
- publication-oriented
- scientifically credible
- logically stable

---

# Revision Example 3

## User Feedback

"Undergraduates may not understand this slide."

---

## Revision Interpretation

Possible problems:

- terminology overload
- excessive abstraction
- weak visual explanation
- high cognitive load

---

## Revision Actions

AI should:

- simplify terminology
- enlarge diagrams
- improve visual explanation
- add progressive annotations
- reduce simultaneous concepts

Avoid:

- removing core concepts entirely
- excessive simplification
- unrelated redesign

---

## Expected Result

The revised slide should feel:

- more intuitive
- easier to follow
- visually guided
- cognitively lighter

Students should understand
the key concept quickly.

---

# Revision Stability Rules

During revision preserve:

- narrative continuity
- visual consistency
- audience adaptation
- typography consistency
- color semantics
- presentation structure

Local revision is preferred
unless global redesign is requested.

---

# Case Philosophy

Presentation revision is not
continuous redesign.

It is:

iterative cognitive alignment
between user intention
and communication effectiveness.
